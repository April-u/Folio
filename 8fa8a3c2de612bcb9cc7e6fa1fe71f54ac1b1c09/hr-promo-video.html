HR Promo Video

Client Project | 9 Feb 2023

Project Details

I was given 40 hours to work on this urgent project for the Client, who needed to present his pitch to company executives.

For the first 6 hours, I spent it looking over the provided powerpoint presentation and script. I revised them to fit the timeline they wanted (their script is about 4 minutes long and they wanted it to be much shorter for a talk). So I drafted the storyboard to see which verbal elements could be communicated visually to save time, while also keeping in mind that the script should be able to be sufficient as-is for accessibility (visual-impaired). Then I recorded my audio and researched stock images. To save time, I screen-recorded essential parts of the video.

For the next 7 hours, I laid out and prepped my animation scenes from the storyboard then continued creating the visual assets and screen recording. I tried to save time by recycling and setting up some of my old assets for the chat animation templates. Sometimes I found great youtube tutorials with downloadable templates I could use for free. Then I had to create the Calendar Fitbit logo since our Client did not have one yet.

The next day I finished the chatbot scenes, re-did the screen recordings and edited the assets (corrected names and dates, removed sensitive information, using the inspect element web console or blurring/masking from the video). Polished the email template and revised its contents to match the story on the video. Added an additional audio recording for an awkward sound break on scene 3 then finished polishing Scenes 2-6. I sourced out the background music on the last minute to present the first draft to the Client.

For the next 7 hours, I polished the remaining Scenes 7-8 and watched tutorials and recycled assets from old projects again. Then I finished rigging and working on the Scene 1 character animation. Then I added the credits.

For my remaining hours, I worked on Client revisions. The Client wanted a trimmed version removing Monika's narrative and showing their target projections only. Then the Client wanted to somehow show the types of automation they wanted to showcase in one scene, which I did for him. Then it was time for him to present.
