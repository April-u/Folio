Musculoskeletal Campaign

Product Client Project | 23 July 2022

Project Details

I was tapped by a Project Manager to work on a returning client for a new video project. From the script they provided, we estimated the duration of the video to last for about 8 minutes. I requested for 4 weeks to work on the project. They revised the script to 6 minutes and provided the recorded the audio. In turn, I was given 2 weeks to work on the video and this time, I worked with my LXD partner for the storyboards. They left the portion of the script they cut out and I was able to rewrite it and create a shorter version for a second video which the client was happy to use as a post-event video.

The Story

Product Client Project, 23 July 2022

Challenge

The client created a script that required a longer video which did not fit in their budget, so I was given less time to work on the video while the client also had to shorten their script. The project also went through a transition of PMs. The Client was also specific about the imagery used, requesting for diverse and inclusive footage as much as possible, and avoiding too much skin. The subject of the video was also highly technical, most of the video assets they require cannot be covered by our basic Adobe Stock license.

Role

I was able to provide honest time estimates for the Client to be informed of the cost of their request and adjust accordingly. Since we compromised on both sides, the Client having reduced scripts and me having shorter timelines, I knew that I needed to save time and decided to avoid the previous time-consuming vector animation style approach to a more video-edit footage montage approach with minimal animation. I also tried to fill in the new PM of changes and discussions exchanged when he was not yet part of the project.

Since the assets required for the video was expensive, such as medical animations of Musculoskeletal movements, I sourced out free 3D assets and rendered the 3D video animations that were not freely available in video format. I also made use of free video sites and added video credits at the end of the video to ensure compliance to CC 4.0 license.

Business Impact

When the Client requested the PM for 8 video translations, I requested additional hours and we were granted additional budget and time to work on the 8 translations. Since the narrators were not professional voice actors, I had to make sure to clean up all their audio and try to keep the quality consistent across all languages. The client also requested that the translators be allowed to pace themselves comfortably, without timing their audio to the video, which required me to edit the video for each translation provided, resulting in differences in video duration. The translators commended the video editing work.
