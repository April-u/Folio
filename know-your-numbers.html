The Story

Comprehend Client Project | 23 Mar 2022

Challenge

The Client specifically requested for colorful palettes, light themes, positive and diverse/inclusive imagery, and creativity. However, the project had a short development turnaround and must still comply with Carbon and use Moodle as it's core structure. I had only 2 weeks to work on the 4.5-minute long video itself. I was given additional time to also work on the site mockup and met with the client to discuss further change requests.

Role

I had limited time to work on the videos and since the storyboard the Client provided only suggested visual ideas in a few slides, it was not enough to cover the 4.5 minute long video. I took the initiative to re-design the storyboards with Carbon design and add more informative visual cues and elements to keep it visually stimulating.

Since the Client and PM wanted a creative and colorful site and since the Moodle LMS is a little limited, I consulted with our Front-end Developer who informed me of a plugin the Comprehend Team has developed to override the Moodle front-end constraints. I was able to get approval to use the plugin for the project from our Squad Leader, Gel Dy, and we were able to see the creative lay-outs into development.

Since the time was limited, I was also able to request for another designer, my partner LXD, Lara Camacho, to be onboarded on the project and work on the wireframes for the site to have more time for the video production.

Business Impact

The Squad Leader, Janette Crouse, reported that the Client was absolutely 'blown away' by the Know your Numbers video. He said he expected something average and got a 'world class' product - the exact words from the customer. The Client returned for another video project, the MSK Campaign. On top of this, we were able to create the colorful, light and creative mockups the Client wanted while sticking to the IBM Brand System, Carbon, by working around the limitations imposed by the Moodle Bootstrap lay-out and re-using existing solutions/plugins our developers had developed.
